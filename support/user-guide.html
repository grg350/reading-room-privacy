Welcome to the Curated Book Coves App

This app helps you find, borrow, and enjoy books from your library.

1. Getting Started

  • Sign up
  • You’ll need:
    • Your name
    • A real email address (yours or an adult’s)
    • A password you can remember

  • A code will be sent to the email. Type the code into the app to finish.

  • Log in

  • Open the app.
  • Type your email and password.
  • Tap Log in.

2. Finding Books

  • Browse
  • Tap Books.
  • Scroll to see all books.

  • Tap a category (like “Fantasy” or “Science”) to see books in that group.

  • Search

  • Use the search bar at the top.
  • Type a title or author name.

  • Tap the search button.

  • Book details

  • Tap any book to see:
    • The cover
    • What it’s about (description)
    • How many copies are available
    • What other readers thought (reviews)

3. Borrowing a Book

  • Open the book you want.
  • If a copy is available, tap the Borrow button.
  • Go to your real library (or follow your librarian’s rules) to pick it up or start reading as instructed. Or wait for email about the delivery/pick-up of the books.

If you don’t see a Borrow button, there might be no copies left right now. You can try again later or pick another book.

4. Returning a Book

When you are finished reading:

  1. Bring the book back to the library (if it’s a real copy).
  2. In the app, go to My books or Your borrowals.
  3. Find the book and tap Return (or the button that says you’ve finished).

A librarian may still need to check the book in on their side, so always return the real book too.

5. Writing Reviews

You can tell others what you thought of a book.

  • Open the book.
  • Look for the review section.
  • Give it stars (for example 1–5).
  • Write a short message, like:
  • “I loved this because…”
  • “Good for kids who like…”
  • “I found this hard because…”

Be kind and respectful. Don’t use rude words or say mean things about people.

6. Recommending New Books

If you read a great book that isn’t in the app:

  • Go to the Recommendations section (if your library has it).
  • Fill in:
  • Title
  • Author
  • Why you think the library should add it.
  • A librarian will look at your idea and may add it to the library.

7. Points and Badges

The app may give you points for:

  • Reading books
  • Writing reviews
  • Making helpful recommendations

You might also see badges or a leaderboard.

These are just for fun—to celebrate your reading.
Try to read more, not just to get points, but to enjoy new stories and learn new things.

8. Staying Safe

  • Keep your password secret.
    Only share it with a parent or guardian if they help you.

  • Use kind words.
    In reviews and recommendations, always be polite.

  • Ask for help.
    If something looks wrong, or you see something that upsets you, tell:

  • A parent
  • A librarian

Happy reading!
